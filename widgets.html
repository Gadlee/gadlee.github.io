Background and problem statement

Agriculture is the backbone of Uganda’s economy, employing over 70% of the population and contributing significantly to the country’s GDP. However, most of Uganda’s farmers are smallholder farmers who rely on traditional practices, word-of-mouth advice, and local observations to make critical agricultural decisions.

Despite growing access to mobile phones and internet connectivity, farmers still face a lack of structured access to market information, pricing trends, and reliable forecasting tools. This limits their ability to:

  • Know the best time to plant or harvest based on market demand.
  • Identify which market or region offers the best price for their produce.
  • Anticipate price drops, demand surges, or export trends that affect profitability.

As a result, many farmers:

  • Sell their produce at low prices due to lack of market access.
  • Plant late or early based on guesswork, leading to mismatches with peak demand seasons.
  • Rely on middlemen, who often offer unfair prices and limited options.
