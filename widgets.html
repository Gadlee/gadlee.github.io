Agriculture is the backbone of Uganda’s economy, employing over 70% of the population and contributing significantly to the country’s GDP. However, most of Uganda’s farmers are smallholder farmers who rely on traditional practices, word-of-mouth advice, and local observations to make critical agricultural decisions. Despite growing access to mobile phones and internet connectivity, farmers still face a lack of structured access to market information, pricing trends, and reliable forecasting tools. This limits their ability to: 1. Know the best time to plant or harvest based on market demand. 2. Identify which market or region offers the best price for their produce. 3. Anticipate price drops, demand surges, or export trends that affect profitability. As a result, many farmers: 1. Sell their produce at low prices due to lack of market access. 2. Plant late or early based on guesswork, leading to mismatches with peak demand seasons. 3. Rely on middlemen, who often offer unfair prices and limited options.
